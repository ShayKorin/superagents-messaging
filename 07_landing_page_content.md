## Page Architecture

Follows the "Varied, Engaging Page" structure from the copywriting skill:

```
1. HERO                     — What it is, why it matters, first CTA
2. SOCIAL PROOF BAR         — Credibility before skepticism sets in
3. PROBLEM SECTION          — Make the reader feel seen
4. HOW IT WORKS             — Destroy perceived complexity in 3 steps
5. USE CASES                — Specific tasks = specific believers
6. BENEFITS                 — What makes it different (benefits, not features)
7. INTEGRATIONS             — Works with what you already use
8. OBJECTIONS / FAQ         — Handle doubt before it kills conversion
9. FINAL CTA                — Strong close with risk reversal
```

*Notes on the H1 choice:*

- The subheadline layers in the use cases first (inbox, leads, reports, meetings) then removes all objections (no servers, no setup, no maintenance).
- Tested against: "The AI agent that does it all, ready in under a minute" — that headline is good but focuses on speed of setup. "Running right now" focuses on the outcome of that setup (an always-on agent), which is more emotionally resonant.

*Notes:*

## 4. HOW IT WORKS

**Section headline:**
> Three steps. Then it just runs.

**Step 1:**
**Describe what you want**
Write it in plain language — like you're talking to a person. "Follow up on leads that go cold." "Send me a weekly sales summary every Friday." "Keep my calendar from falling apart." No code. No config files. No settings to dig through.

**Step 2:**
**Connect your tools**
One click per integration. Gmail, Google Calendar, Slack, WhatsApp, GitHub — your Superagent connects via OAuth. You authorize it, it's in. Done in seconds.

**Step 3:**
**It runs. You don't have to think about it.**
Your Superagent works around the clock on Base44's infrastructure. Not on your laptop. Not on a server you manage. It handles scheduled jobs, reacts to events, sends you updates — and gets smarter the longer you use it.

---

*Notes:*
- Each step: simple verb + outcome + objection destroyed.
- Step 3 is the emotional payoff — "You don't have to think about it" is the dream state, not just a feature.
- Deliberately avoids technical language at every turn.

*Headline:* Doesn't sleep when your laptop does.
*Body:* Your Superagent runs on Base44's infrastructure — not your machine, not a server you babysit. It's working at 3am. It's working when you're in a meeting. It's working when your laptop is closed.

**Benefit 4 — Lives where you already are**
*Headline:* No new app to check.
*Body:* Your Superagent meets you on WhatsApp and Telegram. Updates come to you. You don't have to log into a dashboard to see what it did.

---

**Benefit 5 — Secure by design**
*Headline:* Sandboxed. Permissions-based. Managed.
*Body:* Your Superagent only accesses what you explicitly allow. No open ports. No local server. No plain-text credentials sitting in a config file. Base44 handles the security so you don't have to become a sysadmin to use an agent.

---

*Notes:*
- Five benefits, not six. The original page had six in a grid — too many for a reader to absorb. Cut to the five that address the five biggest objections/anxieties.
- Each benefit: headline states the outcome, body explains *why it's true*. Not just claims — mechanisms.
- Benefit 5 (security) directly addresses the trust issues that caused people to abandon OpenClaw.

## 8. FAQ / OBJECTIONS

**Section headline:**
> A few things people ask before they start.

---

**Q: Is it really that easy to set up?**
A: Yes. You describe what you want in plain language — like you're talking to someone. No configuration files, no setup commands, no server. If you can explain a task to a person, you can set up a Superagent. Most people have their first task running in under a minute.

---

**Q: How is this different from ChatGPT?**
A: ChatGPT answers when you ask. Your Superagent acts on its own — on a schedule, in response to events, without you initiating anything. You set it up once. It runs forever. ChatGPT doesn't send your Friday report. Your Superagent does.

---

**Q: How is this different from Zapier or Make?**
A: Zapier runs rules. Your Superagent reasons. It can handle nuance, compose multi-step logic, adjust based on context, and respond to things that don't fit a predefined "if this, then that" structure. And you configure it by describing what you want — not by building a flow diagram.

---

**Q: Will it stay running? What happens if something breaks?**
A: It runs on Base44's infrastructure — not your laptop or a VPS you manage. We handle uptime, updates, and reliability. You don't get a message saying "your agent crashed." It just keeps working.

---

**Q: Is my data safe?**
A: Your Superagent only accesses what you explicitly authorize. It operates in a sandboxed environment. No open ports. No exposed credentials. Base44 is SOC 2-compliant and manages security end-to-end. Your data doesn't go anywhere you didn't say it could go.

---

**Q: Can I control what it can and can't do?**
A: Completely. You define exactly what tools it can access, what actions it can take, and what it should always ask you about before doing. You're in control — we just handle everything under the hood.

---

**Q: What if I want to use it with my Base44 app?**
A: Your Superagent works natively with your Base44 apps — it can read your data, trigger workflows, send notifications, and automate backend operations. Same platform, new layer of intelligence on top.

---

*Notes:*
- FAQ is kept conversational, not formal Q&A style.
- Each answer is 2–4 sentences max. Long answers = doubt that the short answer exists.
- The Zapier comparison answer is important — it's the secondary competitor many professionals already use. We need to address it.
- The ChatGPT answer uses the weekly report as a concrete example, not an abstract explanation.
