# Hero Variant A — Iterations

*Formula: "{Outcome} without {pain point}"*
*Direction: Clean, direct, removes the objection in the headline itself.*
*Audience: Cold traffic — professionals who want AI doing their work but assume it's complicated.*

---

## What we're fixing from V1

Original:
> H1: "An AI agent that handles your work. Without the setup."
> Sub: List of use cases → delivery → friction removal

Problems:
- "your work" = vague
- "Without the setup" feels like a product feature, not an emotional relief
- Sub is structured like a feature list
- The best line ("just describe what you want and it starts") is buried at the end
- No emotional payoff — it informs but doesn't move

The fix: make the H1 feel like relief, not a spec. Make the sub pull, not list.

## Iterations

### A2
> **H1:** An AI agent that does the work.
> No servers. No setup. No weekends lost.

> **Sub:** Tell it what to handle — in plain language, like talking to a person. It connects to Gmail, Slack, WhatsApp, and your other tools, then runs 24/7 on Base44's infrastructure. Inbox summaries, lead follow-ups, weekly reports, meeting prep. All handled. All automatic.

> **CTA:** Get your Superagent
> **Nudge:** Under a minute to set up · No credit card required

**What changed:** "No servers. No setup. No weekends lost." turns the objection removal into a rhythm — three short hits that build. "Weekends lost" is emotional (time cost) vs. technical (setup difficulty). Taps into the specific frustration of people who tried OpenClaw.

> The work it handles.

> **Sub:** Tell your Superagent what to do — follow up on leads, triage your inbox, send your weekly report, prep your meetings. It runs 24/7 on Base44's cloud. Connects to your tools in one click. No installation, no servers, nothing to maintain. Describe it. It starts.

> **CTA:** Get your Superagent

**What changed:** Two-line H1 creates a cause → effect structure. "You describe → it handles" is the entire value prop in six words. Tight. The sub ends on "Describe it. It starts." — two punchy sentences that land the action.

> **Sub:** Handles your inbox, follows up on leads, sends your weekly report, preps your meetings. Runs 24/7 on Base44's infrastructure — not your laptop, not a server you manage. Connects to Gmail, Slack, and WhatsApp in one click. Describe what you want in plain language. It starts immediately.

### A5
> **H1:** Your AI agent handles the work.
> You just describe it.

> **Sub:** Inbox summaries before you wake up. Lead follow-ups that never miss. Weekly reports that write themselves. Meeting briefs delivered 30 minutes before you walk in. All running 24/7 on Base44's infrastructure — with nothing to install, configure, or maintain.

### A6 — Strongest version
> **H1:** Your AI agent, without the part
> where you spend a weekend setting it up.

> **Sub:** Tell it what to handle. It runs 24/7, connects to your tools in one click, and sends updates to WhatsApp or Slack. Inbox, leads, reports, meetings — covered. No servers. No installation. No maintenance. Just describe what you want and it starts.

> **CTA:** Get your Superagent
> **Nudge:** Under a minute · No credit card required

**What changed:** The H1 is conversational and specific about *what kind of pain* is removed. "The part where you spend a weekend setting it up" names the exact frustration — it's not generic "no setup," it's the *weekend* you've imagined losing. That specificity creates recognition in cold traffic who've thought about trying AI agents but been put off. The long H1 is a deliberate choice — it sounds like a person talking, not a tagline. The sub is tight and rhythmic.

## Head-to-Head

| Version | H1 | Strength | Weakness |
|---------|-----|----------|---------|
| A (original) | "An AI agent that handles your work. Without the setup." | Clean contrast | "your work" vague; "setup" feels technical |
| A2 | "No servers. No setup. No weekends lost." | Rhythm, emotional | Leads with negatives — less inspiring |
| A3 | "The AI agent you describe. The work it handles." | Tightest value prop | Slightly cryptic for cold traffic |
| A4 | "Tell it once. It runs forever." | Strong promise, addresses anxiety | "Runs forever" could feel like hyperbole |
| A5 | "Your AI agent handles the work. You just describe it." | Clear mechanism | Sub parallel structure may feel gimmicky |
| A6 | "Your AI agent, without the part where you spend a weekend setting it up." | Specific, human, recognizable | Long headline — needs confident design |

---

## Recommendation

**A6 is the strongest.**

The long headline is the bet. It reads like a founder talking, not a tagline — which is exactly the right register for cold traffic who are skeptical of AI hype. "The part where you spend a weekend setting it up" is devastatingly specific. It proves we know the audience's exact experience without them having to tell us. That's the moment trust gets built.

**If A6 feels too long for design constraints: A4.**
"Tell it once. It runs forever." is the compressed version of the same emotional promise. Pairs well with a sub that does more explanation work.
