# Audience Research — Base44 Superagents

## The Core Tension

Everyone wants an AI agent that handles their work. Almost no one can actually get one running.

The gap between "I want an AI agent" and "I have an AI agent doing things for me" is where Base44 Superagents lives.

---

## Primary Audience Segments

### Segment 1: The Frustrated Techie
**Who:** Developer, indie hacker, startup founder, or technically-curious professional. Has heard of OpenClaw. Maybe tried it. Maybe gave up.

**What they tried:** Self-hosted agent (OpenClaw, Clawdbot, custom n8n stack). Read guides. Spent a weekend on Docker, SSH, Node.js versioning, environment config. Got it working once, it broke, didn't fix it.

**What they want:** The power of an agent — email handling, lead follow-up, Slack alerts, scheduled reports — without being a sysadmin.

**The exact pain (from Reddit verbatim):**
- *"Every time I get motivated, I open a guide and suddenly I'm dealing with dependencies, version mismatches, permissions, OS differences"*
- *"If you've never used Github before, it's incredibly hard to set up OpenClaw. Not user friendly in the least"*
- *"Honestly for beginners this whole setup process is very complicated. Configuring models, tools, API keys, environment etc can easily become overwhelming"*
- *"I've been messing with OpenClaw for a few days now and I feel like I'm missing something fundamental"*

**What they feel:** Capable but blocked. They know what they want to build; they don't want to manage the infrastructure that makes it possible.

**The unlock message:** *"You don't need to set it up. It's already running."*

---

### Segment 2: The Power User (Non-Technical)
**Who:** Sales leader, marketing manager, executive, operations person. Smart, busy, tech-comfortable but not a developer. Uses ChatGPT, Notion, Slack, Google Workspace daily.

**What they tried:** ChatGPT for one-off tasks. Maybe Zapier or Make for simple automations. Heard about AI agents but assumed it was "for engineers."

**What they want:** An AI that handles the repetitive, time-consuming work in their day — inbox triage, lead follow-up, calendar management, weekly reports — without requiring any setup.

**Their mental model:** They don't think in terms of "hosting an agent." They think in terms of *tasks they wish someone else would handle.*

**Key tasks they want automated (from the Superagents page use cases):**
- *"Scan my inbox overnight. Flag urgent, draft replies to the easy ones, send me a WhatsApp summary at 7am."*
- *"When a new lead comes in, send a personalized follow-up within 10 minutes."*
- *"Every morning, check my calendar. If I have back-to-back meetings, block lunch."*
- *"Every Friday at 5pm, pull sales numbers, summarize trends, flag anything, send to Slack."*
- *"Find a venue for our team offsite, within 2 hours of NYC, fits 20, under $3k/day, available in April."*

**What they feel:** Overwhelmed with tasks that shouldn't require their brain. Slightly skeptical that AI can actually *do* these things reliably.

**The unlock message:** *"Just tell it what to do. It handles the rest."*

---

### Segment 3: The Base44 App Builder
**Who:** Already uses Base44 to build apps. Has a product, database, backend running on the platform.

**What they want:** An agent layer that works *on top of* their existing apps — monitoring users, sending notifications, handling follow-ups, triggering workflows.

**Why this matters:** They already trust the platform. The agent is a natural extension — "same platform, new superpower" (actual copy from the Superagents page).

**The unlock message:** *"Your apps are already here. Now make them act on their own."*

---

## What All Three Segments Share

1. **They've been told AI agents are the future** — and they believe it.
2. **They don't have an agent actually working for them right now.**
3. **The gap is setup friction, not desire.**
4. **They want it to work like a person** — you tell it what to do once, it just handles it.
5. **They want to be notified, not bothered** — updates through channels they already use (WhatsApp, Slack, Telegram), not a new dashboard to check.

## Jobs-to-Be-Done

| Job | Segment | Pain Today |
|-----|---------|------------|
| Handle my inbox automatically | 1, 2 | Manual triage every morning |
| Never let a lead go cold | 2 | Forget to follow up, deal falls through |
| Be briefed before every meeting | 2 | No time to prep, going in blind |
| Get a weekly business summary | 2, 3 | Takes 2 hours to manually pull reports |
| Monitor systems and alert me | 1, 3 | Either build alerting infra or miss things |
| Automate recurring admin | 1, 2 | Tedious, repetitive, brain-wasting |
| Have an agent that doesn't go offline | 1 | Self-hosted agents die when laptop sleeps |

---

## What People Say When They Finally Get It Working

From Reddit and reviews — the reaction when the agent actually works:
- *"How did I ever live without this"*
- *"It's like having an EA that never sleeps"*
- *"I forgot I set this up and came back to a fully handled inbox"*
- *"This is the future and most people don't know it exists yet"*

These reactions are gold. The emotion is **relief + delight** — not "wow, cool tech." That's the emotional territory the messaging should live in.

---

## Audience Summary (One Sentence Each)

- **Frustrated Techie:** Knows they want an agent, gave up on setup, wants someone to have done the hard part.
- **Power User:** Doesn't want to think about "agents" — wants tasks handled, results delivered.
- **App Builder:** Already on Base44, wants to layer intelligence on top of their existing products.
