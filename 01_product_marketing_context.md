## Product Overview

**One-liner:** A fully managed AI agent you describe in plain language — it monitors your tools, takes action, and keeps you updated 24/7, with nothing to install or maintain.

**Category:** Managed AI agent

**Product type:** SaaS — individual and team use

**Business model:** Base44 platform subscription (credits-based)

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Professionals overwhelmed by recurring manual work — inbox triage, lead follow-up, meeting prep, weekly reports. Smart, busy, not necessarily technical. Uses ChatGPT but has never had an agent that actually *runs*.

**Secondary:** Technically-curious users who tried OpenClaw or similar self-hosted agents, hit setup friction, and bounced. They know what they want — they just don't want to manage infrastructure.

**Tertiary:** Existing Base44 app builders who want to add an autonomous intelligence layer on top of their apps.

## The Core Problem

Everyone wants an AI agent doing real work. Almost no one has one running. The gap between "I want an agent" and "I have an agent doing things for me" is almost entirely caused by setup friction.

**OpenClaw (the main alternative) requires:** Docker, Node.js 20+, VPS, SSH access, firewall configuration, SSL certificates, API key management, and ongoing security patching. 4–8 hours to set up. Breaks when the laptop sleeps. Real verbatim from users:

> *"It's been 124 years and I'm still trying to set it up"*
> *"I've quit halfway through more than once. I wanted to experiment, not turn my weekend into infrastructure debugging."*
> *"In 2026 we shouldn't have to be an engineer to get shit done well."*
> *"I installed it but can't keep it alive for more than a day."*

**Zapier/Make fall short because:** Rules-based, not intelligent. Can't handle nuance. Still require technical configuration.

**ChatGPT/Claude fall short because:** Reactive — only respond when asked. No scheduling, no autonomous action.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push** (why they leave the status quo):
- Recurring tasks eating 30–60 min/day
- OpenClaw setup still broken after a weekend
- Leads going cold; reports never getting written
- The Friday report ritual they dread

**Pull** (why Superagents attracts):
- "Tell it once, it handles it forever"
- Specific use case matches their exact life
- "Under a minute" — credibly fast
- WhatsApp/Telegram delivery — no new app

**Habit** (what keeps them stuck):
- "I'll figure out OpenClaw eventually"
- Manual routines on autopilot
- Partial solutions (Zapier, calendar reminders)

**Anxiety** (what worries them):
- "What if it does something wrong?"
- "Is my data secure?"
- "Will it actually work in practice or is this another AI toy?"
